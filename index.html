About me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at Rice University working with Dr. Marina Vannucci and Dr. Simon Fischer-Baum. Currently we are working on characterizing the relationship between brain damages and reading disorders in stroke patients are recovered over years through the process of neuroplasticity. I am now also working with Shell as a data scientist doing spatial-temporal modeling on oil exploration.

I was a PhD student in Statistics and Applied Mathematics of Jack Baskin School of Engineering at University of California, Santa Cruz, advised by Dr. Raquel Prado. Before enjoying Californiaâ€™s sunshine, I studied Economics and Statistics at Indiana University Bloomington (IUB). My PhD research focused on Bayesian spatio-temporal modeling and Bayesian variable selection with applications in brain imaging, fMRI data in particular. I am also in the space-time research group of Professor Hernando Ombao at KAUST and Michele Guindani at University of California, Irvine. My other interests include RStudio tools, data visualization and high dimensional problems.

Selected Publications

Bayesian Spatial Modeling via Kernel Convolutions on Complex-Valued fMRI Signals

A novel complex-valued spatial model via kernel convolution and corresponding MCMC algorithm are developed for detecting fMRI brain activation at the voxel level.
Cheng-Han Yu, Raquel Prado, Hernando Ombao, Daniel B. Rowe
2018
PDF Custom Link

A Bayesian Variable Selection Approach Yields Improved Brain Activation From Complex-Valued fMRI

We present a complex-valued EM variable selection algorithm that leads to fast detection at the voxel level in CV-fMRI slices and also consider full posterior inference via MCMC.The proposed approach leads to activation in the expected motor-related brain regions and produces fewer spurious results than other methods for CV-fMRI.
Cheng-Han Yu, Raquel Prado, Hernando Ombao, Daniel B. Rowe
In JASA A&CS, 2018
